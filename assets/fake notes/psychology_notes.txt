Cognitive Psychology Notes

Memory:
- Encoding: The process of converting information into a form that can be stored.
- Storage: Maintaining information over time.
- Retrieval: Accessing stored information when needed.

Types of Memory:
- Sensory Memory: Very brief storage (milliseconds to seconds)
- Short-Term Memory: Holds about 7±2 items for ~20 seconds
- Long-Term Memory: Potentially unlimited capacity

Forgetting:
- Decay Theory: Memory fades over time
- Interference Theory:
  - Proactive: Old info interferes with new
  - Retroactive: New info interferes with old

Learning:
- Classical Conditioning: Associating two stimuli (Pavlov’s dogs)
- Operant Conditioning: Learning through rewards and punishments (Skinner)
